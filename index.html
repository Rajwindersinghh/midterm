Life of a Starfish

Starfish begin life as little larvae that float openly in the water. As they grow older, they also move to the ocean floor where their bodies can be seen to have radial symmetry. Starfish, unique to most animals, have five arms that spread from a centralized disk, providing it a beautiful star-like appearance. The capacity of starfish to grow more arms is one wonderful quality. Starfish are great masters of regeneration because they are capable of creating a new arm if one is lost due to harm or attack. They are able to live and grow despite serious injuries because of this unusual adaptation. Starfish are efficient predators as well. They are able to capture and control their target due to the specific tube feet they have on the inside of their arms. Their strong arms and tube feet can be used to break apart shells, particularly those of clams and mussels. The starfish splits up its shell and pushes its stomach into its prey, where it physically digests it. These fascinating creatures are attractive because they come in a variety of sizes, shapes, and vibrant colors. Fact about Starfish

  • Starfish are basically echinoderms, not fish.
  • Starfish are found in approximately 2,000 different species.
  • Starfish can be grown again with lost arms.
  • Starfish are able to move and grab prey with the support of their tube feet.
